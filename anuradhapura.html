Anuradhapura


Sacred city of Anuradhapura


This hallowed city was built around a cutting from the Buddha's fig tree, the 'tree of enlightenment,' which was transported there in the 3rd century B.C. Sanghamitta, the founder of a Buddhist nuns' order After an invasion in 993, Anuradhapura, a Ceylonese political and religious capital that existed for 1,300 years, was abandoned. The magnificent site, with its palaces, monasteries, and monuments, has been hidden for many years in impenetrable jungle and is now open to the public.
