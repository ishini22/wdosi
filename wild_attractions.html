Wild Life Attraction In Sri Lanka





Udawalawe



Udawalawe is one of Sri Lanka's more understated national parks, with a significant concentration of wildlife over its broad savannah plains, the most notable of which is its big and steady elephant population. It was founded in 1972 with the goal of protecting a reservoir whose waters are crucial to the parched south-eastern zone's farming populations. The park, which covers 199 square miles, now preserves the rivers at its heart.


Hikkaduwa Marine National Park



>p>Hikkaduwa National Park is one of Sri Lanka's three maritime national parks. A bordering coral reef with a high degree of biodiversity may be found in the national park. On May 18, 1979, the region was designated as a wildlife sanctuary, and on August 14, 1988, it was upgraded to a nature reserve with expanded land area.





Yala National Park



Yala is located in Sri Lanka's south-east, bordering the magnificent Indian Ocean.The world's largest concentration of leopards, stately elephants, sloth bears, sambars, jackals, spotted dear, peacocks, and crocodiles are among its more notable residents.The park is divided into five blocks and covers over 130,000 hectares of land, including light woods, scrubs, grasslands, tanks, and lagoons. Currently, two blocks are available to the public.


Sinharaja Forest



Sinharaja is the country's last surviving primary tropical rainforest, located in south-west Sri Lanka. More over 60% of the trees are unique to the area, and many are considered uncommon. There is a lot of endemic fauna in the reserve, notably birds, but it also has almost half of Sri Lanka's endemic animals and butterflies, as well as a lot of insects, reptiles, and unusual amphibians.



Sri Lanka's Big Five



Big Five Image Specification
Big Blue (Blue Whale) The Blue Whale is the world's largest animal that has ever lived.
Big Tooth (Sperm Whale) The Sperm Whale is the world's largest toothed whale species.
Big Cat (Leopard) The leopard is Sri Lanka's largest cat, the third largest in Asia, and the island's top predator.
Big Ele (Asaian Elephant) The Asian Elephant is Asia's largest terrestrial animal.
Big Tropical Bear (Sloth Bear)) One of the largest bears in the tropics. Although the Sloth Bear may be the biggest bear in tropical latitudes, evidence on Asian bears is insufficient to be definite at the time of writing.
